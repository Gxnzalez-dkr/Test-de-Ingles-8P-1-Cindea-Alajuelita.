🌿 Costa Rica: A Leader in Environmental Conservation

Costa Rica is a small country located in Central America, but it is internationally recognized for its extraordinary commitment to protecting the environment. Although it covers less than 0.03% of the Earth's surface, it is home to nearly 5% of the world's biodiversity, including thousands of plant, bird, and insect species.

The country has established a network of national parks and protected areas that cover approximately 25% of its territory. These areas serve as vital habitats for endangered species such as the jaguar, the tapir, and several types of sea turtles that nest on Costa Rican beaches every year.

Costa Rica has also become a model in the use of renewable energy. In recent years, the country has generated over 99% of its electricity from clean sources, including hydroelectric, geothermal, and wind power. The government continues to promote sustainable tourism as a key part of the national economy, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to enjoy its rainforests, volcanoes, and wildlife.
